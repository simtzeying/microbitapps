About the Project

Empowering STEM Education with Accessible Technology

The micro:bit Apps – Sunway initiative began as a collaboration between Lancaster University (UK) and Sunway University (Malaysia), united by a shared vision: to make high-quality STEM teaching and learning accessible to schools with limited resources.

The project leverages the BBC micro:bit — an affordable, pocket-sized programmable computer — as the centrepiece of real science experiments. Students use it not just to code, but as a scientific instrument: a magnetometer, accelerometer, sound sensor, thermometer, and ammeter — all in one device.

Hardware development and support is provided by Cytron Sdn. Bhd., a leading Malaysian electronics company, ensuring the hardware platform is locally relevant, cost-effective, and teacher-friendly.

Each experiment is carefully mapped to the Malaysian secondary school curriculum (KSSM), spanning Form 1–5 across Science, Physics, and Biology — giving teachers a plug-and-play resource that aligns directly with what students need to learn.

For Teachers

How to Use This Platform

Get your class running micro:bit experiments in four simple steps.

01
Get a micro:bit
Each student or group needs a BBC micro:bit v2. These are available from Cytron Sdn. Bhd. at an affordable price for Malaysian schools.
02
Choose an Experiment
Browse the experiment library above and select one that matches your form and chapter. Each card shows the curriculum alignment at a glance.
03
Load the App
Visit microbit-apps.org on any computer, select the corresponding app, and flash it to the micro:bit via USB — no coding required.
04
Run & Reflect
Follow the step-by-step guide in each experiment card. Use the reflection questions to spark discussion and deepen student understanding.
